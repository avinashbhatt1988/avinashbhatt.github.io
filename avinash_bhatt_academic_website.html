About

Dr. Avinash Bhatt received his PhD in Earth Observation and Disaster Monitoring from National Institute of Technology Uttarakhand in 2025. Research Statement

My research focuses on advancing Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) remote sensing through physically consistent scattering decomposition models, Polarimetric SAR (PolSAR) analytics, and Differential InSAR (DInSAR) based deformation monitoring. During my doctoral work, I developed improved hybrid‑polarimetric decomposition frameworks for robust land cover classification, particularly for complex terrain and heterogeneous urban environments.

My current research direction aims at integrating Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning with SAR observables and decomposition features to enable automated and high‑accuracy Earth observation systems. I am particularly interested in developing interpretable ML models for SAR classification, multi‑temporal deformation analysis, and disaster monitoring applications such as landslides and subsidence.

Future research goals include: (i) AI‑assisted PolSAR decomposition, (ii) multi‑sensor data fusion using SAR and optical data, (iii) explainable AI for remote sensing, and (iv) operational disaster monitoring systems using satellite SAR time series.
